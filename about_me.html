a brief introduction

I'm an incoming senior studying bioengineering and EECS (electrical engineering and computer science) at the University of California, Berkeley. My interests lie within the intersection of these two fields for the development of medical devices. Through the device industry, I aim to overcome unmet clinical needs in low resource settings by working closely with clinicians, engineers, and scientists. I am an undergraduate researcher at the Roy Lab, working as the lead engineer for Fractal.

My previous projects have focused on various aspects of electrical engineering, from image compression and communication to building out two-stage op amps and building a non-invasive diagnostic device.
